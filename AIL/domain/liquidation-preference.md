# Liquidation Preference

## What It Is

When a startup is acquired or exits, investors don't all get paid equally at the same time. Early investors (those who came in first, e.g. seed or Series A) have **priority claim** on the proceeds — they get paid out before later investors, and before founders.

This is called a liquidation preference. It protects early investors who took on more risk by investing when the company was less proven.

Example: if a startup sells for $10M and early investors are owed $8M under their preference, they get their $8M first. Only what's left goes to others.

## Why It Matters for Alfie

Alfie serves institutional investors and family offices who may be LPs (limited partners) in funds or direct investors in startups. Understanding payout structure helps Alfie contextualize investment documents, capital call notices, and fund performance data.

## Key Terms

- **Liquidation preference** — the right to be paid before others upon exit
- **LP (Limited Partner)** — investor in a fund, not involved in day-to-day decisions
- **GP (General Partner)** — fund manager who makes investment decisions
- **Exit** — acquisition, IPO, or wind-down of a company
- **Payout waterfall** — the ordered sequence of who gets paid and when
