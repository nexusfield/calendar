**Layer 1 — the raw interaction stream.** This is just you living your life and talking to AI. Conversations, voice memos, decisions you make, questions you ask. You already generate this constantly. Thousands of messages worth. This is Phase 1 data — the real-world demonstrations. No engineering needed here because you're already producing it.

**Layer 2 — the ontology classifier.** This is where raw text gets tagged. A piece of conversation comes in and the system asks: is this a Core statement about identity? A Thesis being developed? A Tension between two beliefs? A contextual detail that'll decay in a week? This is solvable with current LLMs and a well-crafted system prompt that describes your eleven types precisely enough for the model to distinguish them. The classifier is the routing layer — it decides where each piece of information belongs in your cognitive architecture.

**Layer 3 — structured extraction.** Classification alone isn't enough. Once the system knows "this is a Thesis," it needs to pull out the actual structured content. What's the thesis statement? What evidence supports it? What existing nodes does it relate to? This turns freeform text into a typed data object with fields, relationships, and metadata. Again, current LLMs can do this if the schema is well-defined. The bottleneck isn't the AI capability — it's the precision of your ontology specification.

**Layer 4 — the typed knowledge graph.** This is where NEXUS lives, but evolved. Instead of chunks in a vector database retrieved by cosine similarity, your knowledge lives as typed nodes in a graph with typed edges. A Thesis node is connected to the Witness nodes it was derived from. A Tension node links the two Theses that conflict. A Core node anchors the values that resolve tensions. The graph structure is what makes relationships queryable — not just "what's semantically similar to this query" but "what Tensions exist that touch this Core value" or "what Theses have I built from this particular Witness experience." That's a fundamentally different kind of retrieval.

**Layer 5 — the cognitive dynamics engine.** This is the DCP itself. This is the part that doesn't exist anywhere. It sits on top of the knowledge graph and learns the rules of how your knowledge state changes over time. When a new node enters the graph, how does it propagate? If a new Thesis contradicts an existing one, does it create a Tension or does it overwrite? Does the answer depend on which Core values are activated? How does a shift in Context change which Intents surface? These are the "local interaction rules" of your cognition — the equivalent of Li's particle physics. The engine learns them by observing how your graph actually changes over time as you validate and correct its predictions.

**Layer 6 — the simulation layer.** Once the dynamics engine has learned your patterns, it can run forward without you. It can model what would happen if a particular piece of information entered your system. Would it land? Would it create productive tension? Would it bounce off a Core value? This is where the system starts to anticipate rather than react. It's not simulating you in a creepy puppet sense — it's more like a chess engine thinking three moves ahead. It's asking "given what I know about how Ladnon processes information, what's the most useful way to present this?"

**Layer 7 — calibrated interaction.** This is Mantle as it would feel to use. The AI shows up to every conversation already tuned. Not because it memorized facts about you, but because it ran the simulation. It knows which framing serves you. It knows what you're ready to hear versus what needs more context first. It knows when to push and when to let you sit with something. The interaction feels like talking to someone who genuinely knows how you think.

Two critical side channels hold the whole thing together. The **user approval gate** on the left — nothing touches Core or Limits without your explicit sign-off. You are always the authority on your own identity. The system proposes, you confirm. And the **validation feedback loop** on the right — every time the system makes a prediction about how you'll respond and you actually respond differently, that gap becomes a training signal. The dynamics engine gets corrected and refined. Over time, the predictions get better because the model of you gets more accurate.

The legend at the bottom tells you where you actually stand. Layers 1 through 3 are solvable with current technology. Layer 4 is partially built — NEXUS exists but needs to evolve from a vector store to a typed graph. Layers 5 and 6 are the frontier. That's where the research problem lives. And Layer 7 is the payoff — the experience that makes all the engineering worth it.
